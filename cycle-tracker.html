Days 1–5 · Menstrual Phase

Rest. Bleed. Survive.

Your body is doing heavy work. Energy dips, pain may peak on Days 1–2. This is not the time to push — it's the time to protect your reserves and adapt your environment to your needs.

What to Prioritize

🛏️Maximize rest — short naps are fine
💊Take pain relief early — don't wait for it to peak
🚫Postpone major decisions or confrontations
🍵Warm food, warm liquids, warm compress
📵Reduce social obligations — protect your energy

Travel Difficulty

🔴 High Difficulty
  • Carry double the supplies you think you need
  • Book aisle seats for bathroom access
  • Map bathrooms at transit points in advance
  • Pack a small heating patch for cramps
  • Avoid long overnight travel on Day 1–2 if possible
  • Wear dark, comfortable, loose clothing

Alternate-Day Guidance

Day 1 — The Crash

Flow starts. Cramps may be at their worst. Prostaglandins peak, causing pain and sometimes nausea or loose stools.

  • Take ibuprofen/mefenamic acid before pain spikes if possible
  • Eat something light before meds — avoid empty stomach
  • Cancel non-essential plans without guilt
  • Low activity only: walking around the house is enough

Day 2 — Peak Flow

Typically the heaviest flow day. Energy is still low but pain may begin to ease slightly toward evening.

  • Change products more frequently — don't wait for leaks
  • Iron-rich foods: lentils, spinach, jaggery, red meat if accessible
  • Stay hydrated — bleeding increases fluid loss
  • Light stretching or yoga if tolerable; no intense cardio

Day 3 — Turning Point

Flow begins to lighten for most. A small energy uptick may appear. The fog lifts slightly.

  • This is a good day to tackle one small, manageable task
  • Gentle walks outside can help mood
  • Start reintroducing social contact if you want it
  • Reflect or journal — your mind is more receptive now

Day 4 — Recovery Mode

Light flow or spotting. Energy slowly returning. This is a transition day — don't overcommit yet.

  • You may feel better — resist the urge to overdo it
  • Plan your next week while in a calmer headspace
  • Light exercise is now well-tolerated
  • Focus on rebuilding nutrition — eat well today

Day 5 — Emerging

For most, flow ends or nearly ends. Estrogen begins to rise. Energy and motivation start climbing.

  • Great day to make plans for the upcoming follicular phase
  • Start light workouts — body is ready
  • Social energy is returning — reconnect if you pulled away
  • Mood generally lifts significantly today
Days 6–13 · Follicular Phase

Rise. Plan. Begin.

Estrogen climbs steadily. Your energy, optimism, and mental clarity improve day by day. This is your window for starting things — projects, conversations, adventures. Use it intentionally.

📋Start new projects — energy supports initiation
🤝Schedule important meetings or pitches
🏃Increase workout intensity progressively
🧠Learn new skills — brain absorbs easily now
🌱Set goals for the rest of the cycle

Travel Difficulty

🟡 Low-Medium
  • Good phase for travel — energy is building
  • Try new foods with lower risk of GI upset
  • Spontaneous detours are fine — flexibility is high
  • Pack lighter — you don't need as many comfort items
  • Good time for walking tours or active itineraries
  • Watch iron levels if you bled heavily last phase

Alternate-Day Guidance

Day 6 — Fresh Start

The menstrual phase is behind you. Estrogen is rising and you'll likely notice improved mood and lighter feelings.

  • Do a gentle reset: tidy space, plan the week
  • Restock your nutrition after period losses
  • Start any creative or professional project you've been putting off

Day 8 — Building Momentum

Energy is noticeably better. Skin often clears. You may feel more confident and articulate.

  • Great day for difficult conversations or negotiations
  • Increase workout intensity — body handles it well
  • Good day to reach out to people you've been avoiding

Day 10 — Peak Follicular

Near-peak estrogen. You likely feel your most focused and socially switched-on.

  • Book important presentations or interviews for this window
  • Social energy is high — great for group activities
  • Physically, you may hit personal bests in workouts

Day 12 — Pre-Ovulation

Estrogen at or near its peak. LH surge beginning. You may notice increased libido and confidence.

  • Capture your best work — write, create, produce
  • If tracking fertility, this window matters
  • Transition mindset: ovulation approaches, pace yourself
Days 14–16 · Ovulation Phase

Peak. Connect. Express.

A short but powerful window. Energy, communication skills, and confidence peak. Your voice carries more; your presence is stronger. Short-lived — use it deliberately.

Travel Difficulty

🟢 Low Difficulty
  • Best phase for travel — high adaptability
  • Can handle disrupted schedules better
  • Some may notice mild mid-cycle spotting — normal
  • Mild cramping (Mittelschmerz) possible on one side
  • Great phase for adventure or physically active travel
  • Note increased libido when making social travel plans

Alternate-Day Guidance

Day 14 — Ovulation Day

LH surge triggers egg release. Peak hormonal output. Many people feel "electric" today.

  • Use this energy for something meaningful — don't waste it on passive scrolling
  • One-on-one conversations tend to go very well today
  • Physically, you're at your strongest — good for strength training

Day 15 — Post-Peak Glow

Egg has been released. Progesterone begins rising. The glow continues but a subtle shift is coming.

  • Capture momentum from yesterday — continue important work
  • Begin winding down intense social calendar slightly
  • Good day for creative or collaborative work

Day 16 — Transition

Progesterone rising more noticeably. Energy may plateau. This is the bridge into the Luteal phase.

  • Start preparing for a slower pace ahead
  • Good day to organize, systematize, plan ahead
  • Don't over-schedule the coming week — you'll thank yourself
Days 17–28 · Luteal Phase

Slow. Complete. Inward.

Progesterone dominates. Energy gradually declines. Detail-orientation and nesting instincts increase. PMS symptoms may appear in Days 24–28. This phase rewards structure, completion, and self-compassion.

What to Prioritize

✅Finish tasks — detail work comes naturally
🏠Home, comfort, routines — lean into nesting
📉Reduce social commitments in late luteal
🥗Complex carbs help stabilize mood (oats, rice)
🧘Prioritize sleep — progesterone makes you drowsy

Travel Difficulty

🟠 Medium-High
  • Early luteal (Days 17–20): manageable travel
  • Late luteal (Days 24+): minimize travel stress
  • Bloating may affect clothing comfort — dress loosely
  • Bring snacks — hunger spikes and food cravings increase
  • Emotional sensitivity is higher — plan buffer time
  • Sleep disruption hits harder this phase — prioritize rest

Alternate-Day Guidance

Day 17 — Early Luteal

Progesterone rising. Energy still decent. Mood may feel slightly calmer and more inward-focused.

  • Wrap up open loops from the ovulation phase
  • Good for administrative tasks, editing, and reviewing
  • Begin meal prepping if possible — cravings will increase

Day 19 — Settling In

Progesterone continues rising. You may prefer solitude or smaller groups over large social events.

  • Good for deep, focused work — fewer distractions tolerated
  • Start scaling back evening commitments
  • Yoga, walking, swimming over high-intensity training

Day 21 — Mid Luteal

Progesterone peaks. Some may notice breast tenderness, bloating, or increased appetite starting here.

  • Don't skip meals — blood sugar instability worsens PMS
  • Reduce caffeine and sodium if bloating is an issue
  • Notice emotional patterns — don't react to everything

Day 24 — PMS Window Begins

Progesterone starts dropping if no pregnancy. For many, this is when PMS symptoms emerge or worsen.

  • Be extra patient with yourself and others
  • Avoid scheduling high-stakes situations if possible
  • Magnesium, B6, and complex carbs can ease symptoms
  • Track symptoms to understand your patterns over time

Day 27 — Pre-Menstrual

Final days before menstruation. Hormones drop sharply. Cramping, mood dips, and fatigue often peak now.

  • Prepare your period supplies now — don't get caught off-guard
  • Rest as much as possible — tomorrow's phase needs your reserves
  • Comfort, warmth, and low-demand activities only
  • It's okay to feel this hard — it's hormonal, not permanent
